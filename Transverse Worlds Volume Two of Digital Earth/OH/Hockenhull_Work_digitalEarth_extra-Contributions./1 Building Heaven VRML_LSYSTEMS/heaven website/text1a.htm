"It seemed to me that if I was to incorporate in my Architecture all the poetry of which it was capable, then I should study the theory of volumes and analyse them, at the same time seeking to understand their properties, the power they have on our senses, their similarities to the human organism.
I flattered myself that if I went back to the source of all the fine arts I should find new ideas and thus established principles that would be all the more certain for having their source in nature."
Boullée

Manuscript Department of the Bibliotheque Nationale Cabinet des Estrames (MS Français 9153 and Fol. Ha 55-57) "Considerations Sur L'importance Et L'Utilité De L'Architecture Suivies De Vues Tendantes Aux Progress Des Beaux Arts"

This web site is the repository and archive of a research and production process that questions the suppositions of architecture. 

The central 'product' of this process has been the creation of a 104 minute Beta-Sp broadcast video - 

Below are notes on the work, a collection that serves as an artist statement, as well as links to movies, panoramas, and VRML worlds, connected to the process/production . Also included are some pertinent reference links.

"Building Heaven, Remembering Earth: 
Confessions of a Fallen Architect" 
____________________________

A poetic, visual and musical essay on the confines of architecture.  

Produced, written, directed, and edited by Oliver Hockenhull.  

Music by Lisa Walker, additional Music by Dennis Burke.  

Versioned for broadcast (104 minutes/Beta-SP) and home video release and as a 5 channel video installation
