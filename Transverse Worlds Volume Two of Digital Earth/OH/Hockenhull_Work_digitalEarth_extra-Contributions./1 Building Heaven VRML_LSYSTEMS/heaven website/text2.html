THE DOOR IN THE FRAME/THE DIGITAL VIDEO PRODUCTION

The work became a diarist account, having the feel of an 'on the go' millennium-end tour of some of the world's architectural sites . The intent was to create an essay about the complex of architecture, in its social and ideal forms. The work, of necessity, is cross cultural, situating itself through a nomadic and trans historical perspective into a future of associative cultural references. The work is an expansive, real and imaginary journey, at times self reflexive (and thus questioning) of its own panoptic complicity.

The document is a plenitude of investigation; at times a roaming leisurely reverie, at other times or modalities a maze of alhambraic intensity and transparency, a mirroring of human urgency in the face of crisis - personal and cultural.

____________________________

I had thought this study was about architecture and nature and then I thought it was about homelessness, a wanderer knowing he may never have a home and then I realized that what I wanted to talk about was the lack of coherence and meaning in the world around me - the world that is in Lewis Mumford's still caustic words is a world of "car dominant streets, blank glassy buildings - glares of competitive architectural advertisements, the studied monotony of high-rise slabs, ...buildings and quarters that lack any aesthetic identity and any human appeal except that of superficial sanitary decency and bare mechanical order."

____________________________

I searched for an original dwelling, the unique womb of knowing and unknowing, I travelled to the prehistoric rock caves of Bhimbetka in Madya Pradesh, I went to the works of Palladio, to the Pantheon...

Here too I recognized a sameness, not the monotony of modernism - but the everywhere dream of the human - a creative reading and production built out of negotiations - cultural and personal - with survival, with nature, with the libidinal, and with the unconscious and liminal.

____________________________

Our buildings reflect a machine ideology of straight lines. Their economy is a lie in the face of the wealth and complexity of the real of nature. Our buildings robotosize the human - surrounding in a world of predictability which is also a lie and perversity to the uncertainty (a fated romance) of the world.

We have given over our own evolutionary potential to the machine.

Not the envisioned future machines that are an integrate with the chaotic and singular system of the world, but the pocket calculating machine that slices and dices the eternal, a machine that allows you to go where ever you want today- as long as it is an office.

We do not acknowledge sufficiently the shadow that sustains the contrast and perspective of an awakened eye.

This shadow radiates from our personal and collective evolutionary past and present. A purely erotic beam, it is at the same time wholly difficult to discern, a formlessness that is the first and final attraction of form.

(The Shiva-Lingum 'fits here')

BODY

Fleeting dream of water, needs, desire and tomorrow - spun from whirls around the sun. Our bodies ever desiring, ever growing, ever dying. This home of flesh, memory, want and ignorance. This home generated by an evolution of an uncanny proposition: life.

Our only home and the original architecture. A momentary skin sounding out the presents of incalculable time and dimension. The machine for living, the unsurpassed interface between will, consciousness and the given monument of the cosmos.

We are adrift in our destiny, the currents that break around us is our living architecture.

This is the labyrinth.
