The principle contended for-the cause of the revolt-and the 
power fought against were precisely the same; but mark the 
difference of the result.  The Americans succeeded and their struggle 
was called a glorious revolution while fame and honor was the 
reward of her heroes -  Canada is now unsuccessful and her struggle 
for independence is called a foul and unnatural rebellion while the 
dungeon chains and the scaffold await the leaders of her forlorn 
hope.  

-A New York newspaper account 
of the failed Canadian Revolution of 1838-  


Entre La Langue Et L'Océan is Hockenhull's take on
the present state of Canada's perpetual identity crises
through a focus on the failed Revolution of 1837/1838 and
on a "Canadien" who was banished to the penal colonies of
Australia for having taken part in the Revolt.

The film is a 95 minute walk along the
border of the historical and the hysterical.

strength of the nationalistic and idealistic tendencies
of this Revolution calling it a mere 'farmers rebellion',
an anomaly in Canadian political narrative that only
proves the rule of a compliant population. (The spin
doctors have operated long before television.)-- If there
was no more immediate revolts against Imperialism it's
because Canadians were sensible to authority not because
they were frightened into submission, their houses burnt
and their intellectual leaders hanged or sent to the
antipodes penal colonies1 ...

The value of the nation state is commensurate with its
mythological characteristics.  Meaning the nation state
is equal to its value as a political entity in reference
to inscriptions and historical events that signal of
Justice, Freedom, Equality, Intelligence, and Respect and
the fostering of the potentiality of the individual in
acknowledged responsibility to the collectivity that is
the State through (Direct) Democratic associations. These
Western Enlightenment and Revolutionary notions, this
heritage of a Radical approach to Law and social
structure and function, has not been attained.

The nation state is a redundancy, quaint venues for
maintaining tourist trades and as placebo for  marginal
cultural, social, and technological luddities, --
struggling lumpen proles. International capital
re-configures  itself into newly defined power blocks,
opposition flounders in local struggles  unable to
grapple away the descriptive and prescriptive power  of
the Agenda and its thorough media pedaled persuasiveness.

THE WORLD 

The perpetual war of Capital has come home to
roust as perpetual un and under employment and the
struggle for subsistence, swaying, magnetized under the
invisible velvet gloved iron hand of the New Right- New
Left - the old fascism on technological speed.

Now Individualism = The Pathological Eccentricities of
Everywhere Narcissism.

Corporations have become the rationale of government
existence. People, and the very possibility of
articulation of difference has become subsumed under the
logos of the dominant consumer product/lifestyle. All the
ideals originating with Liberalism and the Democratic
Revolutions that created the Nation States have been
co-opted and incorporated as an aspect of consumerism.

As the Bank advertisement states: "We are concerned with
the only Life that really matters - Yours."

As capital becomes more and more mobile the worker, the
citizen, the definable by place culture and the nation
state itself becomes a side show of the transit and speed
of power.

Every place becomes either a factory or a tourist trap.

St. Benoit surrenders. Jean Joseph Girouard, leader of
this group flees. This is his description of the events
that followed:" A considerable number of the inhabitants
were assembled in my courtyard and two cannon placed in
the gateway were aimed at them while they were told that
they would be exterminated in a few minutes...  There
were no insults and outrages which were not heaped upon
them, no threats which were not made to intimidate them
into declaring the hiding places of those who were called
their leaders.  Thus began scenes of devastation and
destruction more atrocious than any seen in a town taken
by storm and given over to pillage after a long, hard
siege.  After completely pillaging the village, the enemy
set fire to it and reduced it from one end to the other
to a heap of ashes.  They then went in different
directions, ravaging and burning on their way, carrying
their fire as far as the village of St. Scholastique."

In Quebec and in the rest of Canada the defeats of
1837/1838 has had subtle and not so subtle consequences
throughout our collective histories.

They resonate in the animosity between Quebec and the
rest of Canada, between Native and non-Native, they are
reflected in the Canadian well recognized inferiority
complex and the inability of Canadians to control their
own destiny. I am not saying that everything would be
wonderful if the Revolution had been successful, I can
not of course predict an alternative future, however it
would have marked the end of colonial rule through the
establishment of a collaborative English and French
Republican- Revolutionary government.  This would have
been the auspicious beginning of collaboration between
peoples.  It would have, according to the declaration of
(Canadian) independence (yes there was such a
document)given universal suffrage to the Native
population - something that the Canadian government has
only fairly recently granted.  And  importantly it would
have created an environment of certainty as to the
viability of the Nation.

An interesting anecdote - the fate of one Revolutionary
leader---Chénier's heart had been pulled from his body,
examined by the British Military doctors...an autopsy had
been performed at The Black Bull, a tavern situated in a
certain Addison's Hotel in St. Eustache.  They wished to
discover the reason of his death.  He had been shot
outside the Church, falling by the gravestones in the
adjoining cemetery.  He had been the chief rebel of the
St. Eustache Patriotes...

The official story is that everything was in order.
However, war is disorder.  No one can deny that the
British Government condoned the violent reprisals against
the Patriote Villages.

Why did they need to perform an autopsy?  What kind of
morbid curiosity demanded this moment of 'medical'
intervention?  Was it not plain, that the man died from a
gunshot wound and leave it at that...The unofficial
story is that his heart was used as a symbol of
derision...that the British doctors yanked his heart
from his body...that they displayed the heart as an item
of curiosity...that it was carried out in the streets at
the end of a bayonet through the defeated and
terror-stricken village...

The truth of the matter does not matter, because there is
no truth...In the mind of a nation there is only
positions of power and the myths that prop them up.
