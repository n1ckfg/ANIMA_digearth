Time and Black Holes

As Stephen Hawking says in A Brief History of Time, as you cross the event horizon of a black hole you notice no significant change. Things then move faster and faster to the point where you can see the future.

There seem to be more black holes than stars, and our galaxy seems to have its own very large black hole at its centre.

Perhaps our experience of life sped up is a pre-cognition of our destiny: to cross our own event horizon.
