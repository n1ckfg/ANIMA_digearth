de Claude Lamarche-2/NPC






This project called "Trans-Late", is a transliteration of poems (3) I gave with great success at a poetry evening in autumn 1996 here at home, in Saguenay / Lac-Saint-Jean. These poems criticise and joke about Internet, man-woman relationships, the flood of July '96. Their starting theme was "Poétronique" which means poetry versus computer. French words were written in html pages (3 poems=230 pages) because I was showing them on closed circuit, but they were not translated, nor enriched with images and sounds, nor placed on the web either, before I participated in Transverse Worlds. These French poems depend for effect on changes in the word's meanings. These poems work because they each have almost the same written pattern: they work on similarities of sound to extract astonishing meanings that indeed give body and soul. The transliteration is called "Trans-Late": it means that English speakers not fluent in French will gain access to sense at a later point.



As an example of what could be the working process of my poems, here's an english poem made on variations from verbs using the suffix trans:


Trans-Late means that it is never to late to let us translate from language to meanings,

Nor to transcode from thinking to writing,

Nor to transit from french to english,

Nor to transhume from humour to human,

Nor to transact with facts as acts,

Nor to transcribe a script of billions of bits (you bet!),

Nor to transfer fair to affair on the market place,

Nor to transfigure a transfused bloody good man,

Nor to transfix the neck of your friend just to take a steady snapshot of him,

Nor to transform the world in words of works (warm worms),

Nor to transcend sand scripts as sanskrit (Christ!),

Nor to transgress momentaly an agreeable agreement,

Nor to tranship pains and beliefs like frightening freights,

Nor to translite rate codes to a funny babelweb wedding,

Nor to transmit light like a lot of lite stuff of stuffit lite,

Nor to transmogrify the power of love in only love (... alone),

Nor to transmute sound of silence as a muting mute up (... and down... Christ! The birds!!!),

Nor to transpierce sound barrier as guetto blasters,

Nor to transpire hot links out of our work-out session (good old body!),

Nor to transplant heart in soul as art tries on EARTH (Electronic Average Resource Through Human),

Nor to transport portable variable port as portamento to joy,

Nor to transpose sense to sound to image to thought and back to the beginning...

The Logos (Well, login! Have log! Good log!),

And all these vice versa.

Have fun!
